5. Cancellation

5.1 Cancellation by Client. The following cancellation schedule applies:

  • More than 28 days before the Activity Date: full Deposit refund, less a $150 administration fee.
  • 15–28 days before the Activity Date: 50% of the Deposit is refunded. The balance of the Deposit is forfeited.
  • 8–14 days before the Activity Date: the Deposit is forfeited. No further amount is payable if the balance has not yet been paid.
  • 7 days or fewer before the Activity Date (including same-day cancellation): the full Fee is payable. If the balance has already been paid, no refund is given. If the balance has not been paid, it remains due.
  • Rescheduling: the Client may reschedule the Activity Date once at no cost if notice is given more than 14 days before the Activity Date, subject to APLakeside's availability. Only one reschedule is permitted under this Agreement.

5.2 Cancellation by APLakeside. APLakeside may cancel or postpone the Activity where: (a) weather or water safety conditions at Lake Burley Griffin are assessed by APLakeside as making the Activity unsafe or undeliverable (see clause 6); (b) a Force Majeure Event occurs (see clause 7); or (c) the Client has not paid the balance by the due date. In cases (a) and (b), APLakeside will offer to reschedule to a mutually agreed date; if rescheduling is not agreed within 30 days, APLakeside will refund all amounts paid. In case (c), APLakeside may cancel without refund of the Deposit.

6. Weather Policy

6.1 The Activity is designed to operate in all reasonable Canberra weather conditions. Light rain, wind, and cold do not automatically trigger a cancellation.

6.2 APLakeside will make a go/no-go decision no later than 7:00 am on the Activity Date (or earlier where conditions are clearly foreseeable) based on: Bureau of Meteorology forecasts; actual on-site conditions at Lake Burley Griffin; and the assessment of the licensed boat operator.

6.3 Circumstances that will result in cancellation or significant modification include: sustained winds above 30 km/h on the lake; lightning or forecast electrical storm; water safety advisory issued by ACT Parks and Conservation Service; or any other condition assessed by the boat operator as unsafe.

6.4 The Client will be notified by phone and email immediately upon a go/no-go decision. In the event of a weather cancellation, APLakeside will offer to reschedule to the next available date at no additional cost. If rescheduling is not agreed, a full refund of amounts paid will be given.

7. Force Majeure

7.1 Neither Party is in breach of this Agreement for any failure or delay in performance caused by a Force Majeure Event, being any event beyond that Party's reasonable control, including acts of God, pandemic, public health emergency, acts of government (including public health directions or venue closure orders), flooding, fire, or civil unrest.

7.2 The affected Party must notify the other as soon as practicable after becoming aware of a Force Majeure Event. Where the Force Majeure Event prevents delivery for more than 60 days, either Party may terminate this Agreement and APLakeside will refund all amounts paid.

8. Health, Safety and Participant Conduct

8.1 Client's responsibilities. The Client is responsible for ensuring that all participants: are informed of the nature and physical requirements of the Activity; disclose to the Client (for onward disclosure to APLakeside) any medical condition, disability, or mobility limitation that may affect their participation or safety; are capable of participating safely in an on-water activity; and comply with reasonable directions from APLakeside staff, facilitators, and the boat operator during the Activity.

8.2 Life jackets and safety equipment. All participants are required to wear life jackets when on or near the water. Life jackets are provided by the licensed boat operator. Participants who refuse to comply with on-water safety directions may be excluded from the water component of the Activity at APLakeside's discretion, without refund of any portion of the Fee.

8.3 Alcohol. Where alcohol is included in a package, it is provided at the meal or catering component only, not during on-water activities. APLakeside staff and the boat operator reserve the right to decline to serve a participant who appears intoxicated, without liability to APLakeside.

8.4 Participant conduct. APLakeside may remove from the Activity any participant whose conduct is unsafe, threatening, or disruptive, without refund of any portion of the Fee. This includes conduct that contravenes the directions of the boat operator or facilitator.

8.5 Medical emergencies. In the event of a medical emergency during the Activity, APLakeside will contact emergency services (000) immediately and take reasonable first aid steps. The Client acknowledges that APLakeside is not a medical provider and cannot guarantee a medical response.

8.6 Dietary and accessibility requirements. APLakeside will make reasonable efforts to accommodate dietary and accessibility requirements disclosed at least 14 days before the Activity Date. Requirements disclosed after that time are accommodated on a best-efforts basis only.
